# Talking points

- removes local control
- ignores Hayekian local knowledge
- does not compensate for externalities
- possibility for increased littering, theft, trespassing
- top-down control
- no evidence of benefits
- no mechanism to measure adverse impacts
- no mechanism for abutters to collect damages if there are adverse impacts
- allows activities until late into the night (10pm) and very early in the moring (6am)
- explicitly intends for on-street parking
- has no effective upper limit on size ("greater of ... 25% of principle structure") allows for huge stores in, e.g. newly constructed steel industrial buildings, as long as the remainder is dedicated to, say, general storage (put in an 8,000 square foot steel building on a residential lot, use 6,000 square feet as garage and warehouse space, have a 2,000 square foot restaurant in the front)
- no mechanism for repairing damage if predictions turn out to be correct
